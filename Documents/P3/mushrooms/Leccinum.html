Leccinums

Leccinum is a tree-growing mushroom that forms a completely white, coral-like round mushroom that is covered in small spikes. It is firm in texture and has a taste reminiscent of fresh crab with a hint of nutmeg.

Facts

The mushrooms can be baked whole in the oven or divided and mixed into sauces and stews. It is good to fry in slices as an accompaniment to meat dishes. Pom poms are grown in greenhouses and imported mainly from France and the Netherlands, but there is also some production in Norway. Serving tip: Cut into slices and fry over high heat in oil with added garlic and spices. Leccinum is also called porcupine mushroom, Lion's mane and Monkey's head. The Latin name is Hericium erinaceus.

AREA OF USE

Leccinum can be baked whole in the oven, divided and mixed into sauces and stews or fried in slices as an accompaniment to meat dishes.

PROPERTIES

There is no properties on leccinums.

QUALITY REQUIREMENTS

Quality requirements for mushrooms will depend on the individual variety, but as a general rule, mushrooms must be whole and juicy, and free from dark spots and pressure damage. Hat and stem must hang together. The mushroom should be full of juice and have a variety-typical shape. Wild growing mushrooms must be free of snails and other pests.

STORAGE

Storeleccinums in the dark at 0 - 2°C. It provides shelf life of 5 - 7 days.
