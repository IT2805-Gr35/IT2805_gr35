Ceps

Cep, also called boleteus, is a fast-growing mushroom that can become very large in just a few days. It has a light to dark brown cap and a thick, stubby foot that is white or lighter brown than the cap.

Facts

The tubes on the underside of the cap are whitish on young mushrooms and gradually turn yellow. The pulp is firm and white. The taste is mild, sweet and nutty, and very good and suitable for many dishes. Boletus can only be found growing wild. It is prone to attack by insects so careful control is important. Soft consistency is a sign of insect infestation and the product cannot be used. Boletus has a short shelf life in its fresh state, but dried mushrooms can be obtained all year round. Boletus grows in both deciduous and coniferous forests and it grows throughout Norway. Norwegian season from July - October and imports from Sweden, Italy and France from August to November.

AREA OF USE

Mushrooms are generally used either fried, boiled or in a wok. Also common in soups, sauces, stews, stews and as a garnish. Make sure that mushrooms are not boiled or fried for too long. Then it loses some of its flavor.

QUALITY REQUIREMENTS

Quality requirements for mushrooms will depend on the individual variety, but as a general rule, mushrooms must be whole and juicy, and free from dark spots and pressure damage. Hat and stem must hang together. The mushroom should be full of juice and have a variety-typical shape. Wild growing mushrooms must be free of snails and other pests.

STORAGE

Storage in the dark at 0 - 2°C provides a shelf life of 3-5 days.
