Facts

Pineapples actually consist of over 100 individual berries gathered around a woody stem that ends in a crown of leaves. Pineapples are available in Norway all year round in several sizes. Pineapple is a tropical fruit and came to Europe with Columbus. In Brazil it still grows wild. The fruit grows on a stem surrounded by a rosette of leaves and is harvested throughout the year. Pineapples actually consist of over 100 individual berries gathered around a woody stem that ends in a crown of leaves. Pineapples are available in Norway all year round, in several sizes. The smallest are called mini pineapples and are from 2-3 hectares. The largest are sweeter full-grown pineapples of around 2 kilos. The skin of the pineapple is scaly, dry and crispy, while the pulp is juicy and has a fresh and sour taste.

AREA OF USE

With its sweet, sour and fresh taste, it's actually hard to find something that you can't eat pineapple with. It is often used in salads, in desserts, as a snack and it goes well with many meat and fish dishes. It is widely used in strong dishes from Asia, India and Mexico. Pineapple can be prepared in several ways, raw or heat-treated. Grilled pineapple is worth trying.

PROPERTIES

Pineapple has a high content of vitamin C. Vitamin C contributes to the normal functioning of the immune system and protects the cells against oxidative stress. Vitamin C increases the absorption of iron from other foods you eat and contributes to the formation of collagen, which is important for gums, teeth and skin. Vitamin C also contributes to normal energy metabolism. Pineapple also has a high content of dietary fiber which is beneficial for digestion. The properties apply when consuming at least 100 g of the product.

QUALITY REQUIREMENTS

All pineapples are ripe when harvested. The sugar content is carefully measured before it is harvested so that it meets the minimum requirement. A pineapple should be heavy for its size, well-shaped and healthy-looking. The petals must also be green and fresh. The color of pineapples can vary from green to yellow, and a ripe pineapple can be green in color.

STORAGE

8 - 20°C promotes ripening, 4 - 8°C storage of ripe product. Ripe pineapples are stored at 4 - 8 °C, while unripe ones are preferably slightly warmer to speed up the ripening process. The ethylene in the room will also speed up ripening.
