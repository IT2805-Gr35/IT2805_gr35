Cherrys

Cherries are stone fruits and related to plums. Cherries are actually a term that includes two main groups: Sweet cherries, which in Norway are called morels, and sour cherries, which we call cherries here.

Facts

Cherries originally come from the area around the Adriatic Sea, but now grow wild in several places in Europe and other parts of the world. Many also have these fruits on their own trees in the garden. Cherries have a dark red color on the skin and pulp and taste sour. The fruit is therefore mostly used for juicing or other processing.

AREA OF USE

Cherries have a very sour and sour taste and are best suited to be preserved. It can still be eaten plain with yoghurt or in a fruit salad. When processed, sour cherries are suitable for juice, wine and liqueur production. The fruit can also be used for compote or jam.

PROPERTIES

Cherries are a source of beta-carotene and dietary fibre. Beta-carotene is converted into vitamin A in the body. Vitamin A helps to maintain normal skin and normal hair. Dietary fiber is beneficial for digestion. The properties apply when consuming at least 100 g of the product.

QUALITY REQUIREMENTS

Cherries must be clean, whole, firm in the shell without cracks or other damage. They must also be free from damage caused by insects and birds. The shell should be shiny without soft or dark spots. Ripe cherries should have a deep red color.

STORAGE

0 - 2°C Kirsebær har kort holdbarhet og bør oppbevares så nær 0 - 2 °C som mulig, og med høy luftfuktighet.
