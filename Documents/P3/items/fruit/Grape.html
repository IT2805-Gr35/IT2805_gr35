Grapes

The grape is one of the world's most cultivated fruits, and it is also one of the oldest known fruits. There are mainly two types of grapes; wine grapes and food grapes.

Facts

Grapes originally come from Asia where they have been known for over 5,000 years, and are one of the oldest fruits we know. Today, grapes are one of the most cultivated fruits in the world. There are thousands of grape varieties, and it is most common to distinguish between green, red and blue, and between table grapes and wine grapes. Of what is harvested from grapes, only 10% is eaten naturally. A full 80% goes to wine and spirits production and the rest to raisins, juice and canning. Taste, consistency and size vary depending on the variety, and there are also stone-free varieties. Grapes do not ripen after harvesting and must therefore be harvested ripe.

AREA OF USE

Grapes are eaten naturally as refreshment, in fruit salad, with cheese or dessert. Grapes have a nice flavor and go well with poultry and roast meat. Grapes have a wide range of uses and are also decorative on fruit platters.

PROPERTIES

Grapes are a source of dietary fiber. Dietary fiber is good for digestion. The properties apply when consuming at least 100 g of the product.

QUALITY REQUIREMENTS

Grapes must be whole, firm and clean without impact damage or cracks. It should be juicy and stuck to the stem.
