Facts

Edible mushrooms are one of the five safe mushrooms. Maybe even the safest of these, without poisonous mushrooms. The peculiar oblong bell shape and the shells on the hat are completely unique.

There are a number of other cuttlefish, but these are smaller and without a white hat with scales. Only food ink fungi are counted as food fungi. Gray cuttlefish can be eaten, but gives easy poisoning together with alcohol. An effect that can last up to two weeks after the meal.

Edible mushrooms must be prepared quickly, and only fresh mushrooms that are completely white inside can be used. The spores and cap turn into a black liquid – ink – when the fungus is ripe enough. They can be kept from a few hours to several days if they are wrapped in damp paper or a kitchen towel. Can also be placed in water.

Feel free to remove the cap from the style when picking the mushrooms, and store them in a plastic bag to keep them moist. This makes them last longer. The stems do not dissolve and can last longer than the hat.

The ink from the edible ink fungus must have probably been used to write the first Koran.
