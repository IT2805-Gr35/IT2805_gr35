Apples

Apple is a fruit known since the Stone Age and is found today in an unknown number of varieties. The apple originally comes from Asia and we know the fruit from the biblical story, where Eve tempts Adam with a fruit from the tree of knowledge.

Facts

This article is a wild growing grocery and is commonly found in woodlands with lots of other fruits, berries and mushrooms. The whole mushroom is edible and has a good taste that is slightly stronger than mushrooms and with a peppery aftertaste. The food is also sold to large households in a dried state and the color will then be slightly darker.

AREA OF USE

The article can be used alone as a garnish, or together with other foods in a mushroom stew. It can also be fried, used in a wok, or in soups or sauces.

PROPERTIES

It has a low energy and fat content and a high content of niacin, selenium, copper and dietary fibre. Furthermore it is also a source of beta-carotene, riboflavin, iron and potassium. Niacin helps to reduce fatigue and exhaustion, while selenium helps to maintain normal hair and nails. Beta-carotene is converted into vitamin A in the body. Vitamin A helps to maintain normal skin and normal vision. Riboflavin contributes to normal energy metabolism, while iron contributes to normal transport of oxygen in the body. Potassium helps maintain normal blood pressure. The properties apply when consuming at least 100 g of the product.

QUALITY REQUIREMENTS

Quality requirements for fruits will depend on the individual variety, but as a general rule, vegetables must be whole and juicy, and free from dark spots and pressure damage. Hat and stem must hang together. The mushroom should be full of juice and have a variety-typical shape. Wild growing berries must be free of snails and other pests.
